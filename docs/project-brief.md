6. Research Citations — Verified
Both citations are verified as real peer-reviewed research. Use exactly as written.
Citation 1
Venugopal, K. et al. (2024). Alzheimer's & Dementia. Bilingual adults develop dementia symptoms an average of 4.5–5 years later than monolinguals.
Citation 2
Concordia University (2024). Bilingualism: Language and Cognition. Bilingual individuals with Alzheimer's demonstrate significantly greater hippocampal neuroplasticity (larger hippocampal volume) than monolinguals.
FTC Compliance — Lumosity Precedent
Key reference: FTC v. Lumos Labs (Lumosity) — $2M settlement for unsubstantiated brain training claims. MEMOIVA avoids all "will prevent/delay/treat" language.
"Research-informed" ✅ — not "research-validated" ❌
"Inspired by peer-reviewed research" ✅ — not "proven by research" ❌
"Among the first programs of its kind in the U.S." ✅ — not "only program" ❌
"Research associates with greater brain resilience" ✅ — not "will protect your brain" ❌
Required Disclaimer (must appear on all public-facing content)
MEMOIVA Circle is inspired by peer-reviewed research on bilingualism and cognitive health. It is not a medical device and does not diagnose, treat, prevent, or cure any medical condition. The assessments used in MEMOIVA Circle are educational tools, not clinical diagnostic instruments. If you have concerns about your cognitive health, please consult a qualified healthcare provider.

11. Approved About Section Copy — LOCKED
This is the exact approved text. Do not alter without founder approval.
MEMOIVA Circle is the flagship program of RAMP Linguistic Society — a woman veteran-owned language and technology company built on a simple belief: that the brain never stops growing, and that language is one of the most powerful ways to protect it.
It was inspired by her grandmother living with dementia who wanted to learn a new language — and kept trying, with everything she had — at a time when the right tools simply didn't exist.
But now MEMOIVA Circle is bridging the gap and exists specifically for adults 50+ who are navigating cognitive decline, are concerned about their brain health, or are simply determined to keep their minds sharp. And for the families and caregivers who love them.
Grounded in peer-reviewed research on bilingualism and cognitive wellness, it's the program that should have existed for her grandmother — and for every student like her.
Rigorous. Warm. Dignity-forward. Those are the RAMP Linguistic Society and MEMOIVA standards.
In every word. Every session. Every circle.
